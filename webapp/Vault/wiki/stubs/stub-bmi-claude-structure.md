# Bmi Claude Structure

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This is a structured course document from Nagarro designed to equip technology consultants with frameworks for advising BFSI (Banking, Financial Services, and Insurance) clients on digital disruption and business model innovation. It covers how to diagnose genuine disruption, assess platform economics, and architect transformation strategies, progressing modularly from understanding business models to designing strategic responses. The content draws on classic disruption theory (e.g., Christensen's innovator's dilemma with the Digital Equipment Corporation case), value discipline frameworks, and platform business models to help consultants move from observing digital threats to delivering implementation roadmaps.

## Key Claims

- Companies fail when they try to excel at all three value disciplines (operational excellence, customer intimacy, product leadership) simultaneously, rather than by choosing the wrong one
- Two different rates of improvement operate in every market—technology improvement outpaces customer needs—and the gap between them explains why successful companies fail
- Disruptive products don't need to match incumbents' full feature sets; they only need to cross the "good enough" threshold for specific jobs customers need done
- Platforms don't own resources—they orchestrate connections between two distinct user groups who each need the other (e.g., Monster.com connecting job seekers and recruiters)
- Disruption often requires fundamental business model change, not just incremental product improvement, as illustrated by insurance brokers facing aggregator platforms that unbundle information from physical products

## Topics Covered

Disruption theory and the innovator's dilemma, BFSI digital transformation, value disciplines (operational excellence / customer intimacy / product leadership), platform business models and network effects, two-sided markets, fintech competition with traditional banks, insurance industry disruption (Lemonade / parametric insurance / aggregator platforms), robo-advisors and asset management, build-partner-acquire strategy frameworks, business model response design, sustaining vs. disruptive innovation rates, commission-based vs. digital distribution models
