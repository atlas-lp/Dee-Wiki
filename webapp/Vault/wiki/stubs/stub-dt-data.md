# Dt Data

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This source presents a typology of business models organized along two axes: upstream vs. downstream focus and single-firm (focused) vs. ecosystem (integrative) scope, yielding four archetypes — Product, Service, Platform, and Solution. It explores how firms shift from product-based to services-based business models, using cases such as Ping An's ecosystem strategy to illustrate integrative, downstream approaches. The document also examines how investor expectations around growth potential versus current-period profits shape firms' ability to invest in digital innovation and achieve higher market valuations.

## Key Claims

- Business models can be classified into four types — Product, Service, Platform, and Solution — based on their position along upstream/downstream and focused/integrative dimensions
- Firms increasingly shift from standardized product business models toward services business models
- Ecosystem-based business models (like Ping An's) attract consumers through new business lines with large consumer numbers and frequent interactions, then funnel them into traditional business lines
- Investors who value firms for growth potential rather than current profits are more likely to invest in digital innovation and obtain higher market valuations
- Industry incumbents whose investors expect current-period profits are less likely to invest in digital innovation and receive significantly lower market valuations when attempting to become digital leaders
